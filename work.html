Work Experience

My work history has given me opportunity to attain and exhibit many of the qualities that mark a productive and desirable employee of any company. First and foremost, I am a hard worker and do not shy away from uncomfortable tasks, as seen in my record with Harvard Dorm Crew and Georgia Civil. Nor am I uncomfortable when responsible for others, as I have shown by my work with Harvard Summer School. I am a fast learner and am not intimidated by the challenge of a learning curve; my audio system project (on my Engineering page) was built from scratch in four months. My quantitative abilities complement my ability to work well in groups, speak thoughtfully, think critically, and present ideas well.

Harvard Summer School

During the summers of 2015 and 2016 I worked with the Harvard Summer School, serving as proctor during both summers and as an Assistant Coordinator in one of the administrative offices in 2015. My proctor role involved monitoring 30 high school students living in my hall, being in frequent communication with my Assistant Dean, planning weekly social events for my students, diffusing conflicts that arose, completing mid-summer checkups with each student, and assisting in organizing events throughout the summer. When I was Assistant Coordinator, I was responsible for the 25+ trips into the Boston area put on by our office. I finalized logistics, ensured we had tickets, trained trip chaperones, communicated with venue administrators, planned travel, and oversaw most trip departures.

Harvard Dorm Crew

Dorm Crew is a student-run group responsible for preparing dorms for summer school after the end of the spring semester (called Spring Clean Up - SCU) and then preparing them for the fall semester after summer school ends (Fall Clean Up - FCU). I participated in FCU my freshman fall, then SCU for the subsequent three springs. These programs involve working 8 a.m. to 4:45 p.m. in a crew of 10-20 and cleaning an entire dorm building (ranging from 20 to 120 rooms) in one week. The students who run Dorm Crew are called Captains, and each Captain is in charge of a crew. In spring of 2016, I was requested to work with numerous Captains and was the most requested worker among the 200+ students in SCU, indicative of my hard work in previous SCU/FCUs.

Harvard Athletics - Term Time

I worked term-time during my sophomore year as a desk monitor for Harvard Athletic Department in one of our campus gyms. My responsibilities included checking in members and their guests, fielding phone calls about resource reservations, and managing conflicts over court or room space when they arose.

Georgia Civil

During the summer after my freshman year of college I interned at Georgia Civil, a civil engineering firm in Madison, GA. I assisted frequently with land surveying, traveling to numerous work sites across Georgia and using both total stations and GPS methods. In the office, I searched databases for potential building plots for clients of ours, conducted building code research to advise a particular client case, and assisted with administrative tasks when needed.

Benchmark Automation

Benchmark Automation is a food packaging automation manufacturer in Athens, GA, and I interned with them during a summer in high school. While it was part-time, it gave me insight into manufacturing, the types of tools and precision needed to create such large instruments, the myriad ways a computer can detect and manipulate an object on a conveyer belt, and the ingenuity needed to create novel and custom solutions to clients' problems. I worked as an assembly intern, assisting with assembly of the machines and seeing many different sides of the assembly process over the course of the summer.
