Engineering Projects

100 Watt Audio System

From May to September of 2016 I built a 6-piece sound system consisting of a preamplifier, a stereo amplifier, a subwoofer amplifier, a pair of stereo speakers, and a subwoofer. Having never worked with audio circuits nor speaker design of any sort, I spent much of my time researching and learning. An extensive writeup of the process will be available on this page soon.

Gait Analysis Device

One of my classes in spring of 2016 was centered around creating a novel medical device over the course of the semester. However, in addition to the creation of this device, the class focused significantly on researching prior art in various fields, communication with professors and teaching staff, interviewing experts in our chosen area, effective presentations, and designing rigorous evaluation procedures for our device's prototypes. My three teammates and I worked to create a wearable gait monitor that would help prevent injurious runnning in its user. A summary of our work will be available on this page shortly.

Bicycle Safety Circuit

Two classmates and I designed a series of safety-oriented circuits for bicycle riders in spring of 2015. My biggest contribution was the creation of a digital odometer and speedometer based on our wheel-triggered clock signal, but our project also included an LCD readout, cascading 3-light turn signals, and a rear-facing proximity sensor to monitor the distance of traffic.

Adjustable Soccer Rebounder

I recently designed and built a soccer rebound wall to aid in soccer practice. These rebounders can be used by an individual or can be incorporated into team drills, and they can be adjusted to return the ball on the ground or in the air.
